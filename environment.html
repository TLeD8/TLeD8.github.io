How Smartphones Affect the Environment

Databases

  • How they Impact the environment
    Smartphones have a particularly high environmental impact due to their high turnover rate (consumers replace smartphones on an average of two years) and the amount of valuable metals used in them, like lithium, copper, and gold.
  • Global Warming Potential
    The amount of CO2 created by a smartphone throughout its lifespan corresponds primarily to database energy generation: Coal and natural gas for the electricity grid, petroleum for transportation, etc.

    • Manafacturing

    Circuit production, phone shell raw material acquisition and production, and assembly and distribution all contribute to the total Global Warming Potential. The average smartphone made in 2016 produced 45 kg CO2. This is a 34 kg increase compared to 2012 models. Newer smartphones are requiring more and more resources and energy to meet the needs of the market. If we include accessories, like the charger, packaging, USB-cable, headphones, then the CO2 output becomes 68 kg CO2.

  • Battery
    The battery is the most costly component in a smartphone. Not only does manafacturing batteries produce hazardous waste into the environment, but also charging and replacing it takes a huge amount of energy over its entire lifespan.
