How Social Media is Bad for the Environment

Database centers that support social media platforms consume extravagant amounts of energy that lead to CO2 being emitted into the atmosphere, materials needed to build a database require mining metals that destroy entire ecosystems and a power failure in a data center produces hazardous waste in the form of expired batteries.

New Media Frameworks

  • Identity

    Social Media is changing how we view ourselves and how we view others. Being able to freely express a modified version of yourself on the internet offers a new way for younger generations to explore who they are.

  • Social Capital

    Likes and retweets have been given real world value due to there popularity among users. Social media popularity becoming social capital is the result of this, and it's making users more likely to use social media.

  • Digital Divide

    Marginalized groups are not getting access to the internet, and its limiting environmental awareness.

My Solution

Use social media to spread environmental awareness and reduce the time you spend on social media.

  • 470,000,000 people that use Social Media
  • 45kg CO2 from average smartphone usage
  • 42% of social media users experience bullying
  • 49% CO2 emission comes from just smartphone usage

More people than ever are using social media to form their identities, build social networks, and spread there messages. Social media holds many opportunities for people to spread good ideas to anyone around the world. My solution is to promote the dangers of social media and how it can negatively affect mental and physical health. Send a message that makes people aware of the negative environmental impact social media has, and people will be less likely to frequently use social media.
